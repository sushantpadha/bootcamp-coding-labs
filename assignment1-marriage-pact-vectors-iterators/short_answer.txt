Assignment 1: Marriage Pact — short answers
=============================================
Q1. reserve vs resize — and the capacity you chose in get_applicants.
    YOUR ANSWER:
    reserve(n) pre-allocates capacity without changing size so elements aren't constructed.
    resize(n) actually changes the size, default-constructing or truncating elements.
    i used reserve(1000) as a rough estimate from eyeballing students.txt; this
    avoids repeated reallocations as we push_back, without the overhead
    of constructing 1000 empty strings upfront like resize would.

Q2. What does it mean for a std::ranges algorithm to take a projection?
    Give one example from your find_matches (or explain why a projection
    didn't help).
    YOUR ANSWER:
    Projection is a callable applied to each element before the
    algorithm's predicate sees. one common use case is to compare on a member
    without transforming the range itself. in `find_matches` i
    pass `initials` as a projection to `copy_if` (must wrap in lambda for template to be initialized).
    without the projection i'd have to compute `initials` inside the
    predicate and lose the clean separation of "what to extract" vs
    "what to check"

Q3. Iterator invalidation in run_mixer. Which strategy did you choose
    (erase-returns-next-iterator / indices / restart) and why?
    What specifically goes wrong with the naive approach?
    YOUR ANSWER:
    i used index-based traversal with i-- after erasing a pair. the
    naive approach fails because erase() invalidates all iterators at
    and after the erased position.
    with indices, after erasing at j then i (higher index first!),
    i-- corrects for the fact that a new element has shifted
    into position i, so the outer loop's ++i would skip it.

    also, it was quite hard to re-use the previous functions in this
    routine effectively, due to erase issues and the fact that the range had
    to NOT include the original person (i) to be paired.

Q4. at() vs []. When, if ever, would you reach for vec.at(i) over
    vec[i] in this assignment?
    YOUR ANSWER:
    at() does bounds checking and throws std::out_of_range; [] is UB
    on out-of-bounds. in this assignment i wouldn't use at() in the
    hot path since indices are always checked.
    it COULD be used for `name[space+1]` but i covered the bound checking defensively.
